Flying Figures

The "Flying Figure" menu gives you a number of "figures" to use.

There are squares, rectangles, circles, triangles etc. (Check the "Flying Figure" menu for the full list).

One quick way to see what figures are available is to click the numbers on the keyboard (1 to 7 and 0)

One special "figure" is the inner ruler (to select - click "Flying Figure / Ruler" from the menu or press 0 (zero) key)

It is the same as rectangle, but with tickers on it (this is the long waited rotation option everyone wanted from the very first version of Screen Ruler)

To rotate or resize the figure -- drag the control points.

To move -- click inside the figure and drag.

To prevent rotation while resizing: press and hold the Command (Mac) or Alt (Windows) key and drag the control point.

To rotate by 45 degrees: press and hold down the Shift key while dragging a control point.

For each figure the program calculates Area and Perimeter (visible on the figure itself and/or the info panel, depending on the selected View options)

When measuring with these figures, keep in mind that the width / height values include all pixels, including the left / right / top / bottom border.

If you see, for example, the width reported as 200 px, this means that there are 200 pixels from the left to the right, including the 1-pixel borders at left and right.

If you are interested on the precision and the calculations involved (especially when it comes to rotated figures) -- read the "pixel tale" section of this document.
