Guides

As in the photo editors, you can drag guides from the left and top tickers.

To add new guide -- click right over the left or top ticker, hold down and drag right / bottom.

To delete guide -- just drag it out of the ruler.

To hide all guides (without deleting them) -- click "G" (or select View / Show Guides" from the menu.

To clear all guides, click "Clear All Guides" from the View menu (Command/Alt + Shift + G).

One option that could make you feel strange is the "Guides Hover Width / Height" (also in the View menu).

When checked, this will show the distance between every two guides when the mouse is over any guide. The fun part is that, again, the distance includes the two guides. This may look like one more pixel in the readings, but it is on purpose. I wanted to keep things simple, that's why every time when we are measuring distance (width, height, length) we count all pixels. In this case, we count the two pixels (not only one of them) and that's why you will see this additional pixel.

Here is an example:

Set the Start Point to "Top-Left" and check "Ruler Starts with 0";

Drag a guide from the left ticker to pixel number 100 (as shown on the ticker);

Drag another ruler to pixel 200;

Confirm that the "Guides Hover Width / Height" is checked.

Move the mouse over one of the guides.

Now you can see what I mean -- we have two guides (on pixel 100 and 200) but the width shown is 101px.

Usually we don't count the first guide (such as the "zero problem" discussed before), but because these guides are meant to be used to measure distance, I believe it is better to include both left and right guide.
